About

I build and validate numerical experiments to understand how disk physics and orbital dynamics shape the architectures of planetary and multiple-star systems. Recently, while auditing IllustrisTNG merger catalogs, I tracked seemingly unphysical stellar-mass jumps to subhalo mass misassignments near mergers, which made me double-down on algorithmic verification and observation-facing definitions in simulation work. At Berkeley, upper-division courses and the Astronomy Data Lab shifted my mindset to design models with observables, uncertainties, and selection effects in mind.

Orbits in hydro vs. DMO simulations

Advisor: Dr. Lan Wang (NAOC)

Built an end-to-end pipeline to match hosts/satellites and track orbits from infall to coalescence. We find baryons drive more radial trajectories and shift collision-angle distributions toward larger values for massive hosts—implications for stellar halos and streams.
